Blue Dart Frog

Poison dart frogs are also known as poison arrow frogs. They get this name because of the hunters living in the rainforest using the skin toxins on their darts and arrows.

  • Scientific Name: Dendrobates tinctorius azureus
  • Average Length: 2 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 7.6 years
  • Habitat: Tropical rain forests

The blue poison dart frog is easily recognized by its blue color, which is generally darker on the limbs and belly and overlaid with black spots or patches, especially on the head and back. The male blue poison dart frog has obviously widened finger discs compared to the female.
